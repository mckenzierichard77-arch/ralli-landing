Medical disclaimer

Ralli is not medical advice — and we are not your dermatologist.

We built Ralli to help you make more informed choices about the products you put on your skin. But pore scores, ingredient ratings, routine grades, and the things your friends say in the app are educational tools, not diagnoses. They are not a substitute for the advice of a qualified healthcare provider.

Please talk to a dermatologist if:

  • You have persistent acne, eczema, rosacea, or any skin condition that concerns you.
  • You're starting or stopping prescription skincare (tretinoin, hydroquinone, oral medications).
  • You're pregnant or breastfeeding — many active ingredients have specific guidance for these situations.
  • You have an allergic reaction to any product, especially one with swelling, hives, or difficulty breathing — that's a 911 call, not a Ralli search.

A few things to keep in mind when you use Ralli:

  • Pore scores are general guidance, not personal predictions. An ingredient flagged as comedogenic for most acne-prone skin may be perfectly fine for yours.
  • Everyone's skin is different. Two people can use the same product and have completely different results.
  • Always patch-test. Apply a small amount to your inner forearm for 48 hours before using a new product on your face.
  • Ingredient data has gaps. We pull from published research and public databases. Formulations change. If you spot a mistake, tap "Report a correction" — it genuinely helps.
  • Community reviews are opinions, not evidence. A 9/10 community rating means a lot of women loved it; it does not mean it's safe or effective for you.

You're the expert on your own skin. We're here to give you better tools to make decisions — but those decisions are yours.
